# In-house Bank

Slug: `in-house-bank`

## Profile overview

Operates as the group's internal bank — provides banking-like services (deposits, loans, FX, payments) to group entities, replacing or supplementing external banking relationships.

## Functions — topics to cover

- **Internal Deposit Taking**: How the in-house bank accepts deposits from group entities and pays interest.
- **Intercompany Lending**: How the in-house bank lends to group entities (overdrafts, term loans, revolvers).
- **Payment Processing & Netting**: How cross-entity payments are processed and netted.
- **FX Conversion & Hedging**: How the in-house bank provides FX conversion and hedging to group entities.
- **Working Capital Facilities**: How facilities support working capital (receivables financing, inventory).
- **Overdraft Management**: How overdrafts are authorized, priced, and monitored.
- **Interest Rate Management**: How interest rates are set on deposits and loans.
- **Account Administration**: How accounts are opened, maintained, and reported.

## Assets — topics to cover

- **Internal Loan & Deposit Portfolio**: Outstanding loans to and deposits from group entities.
- **Payment Processing Systems**: Systems for payments, netting, and reconciliation.
- **FX & Treasury Systems**: Systems for FX transactions and treasury management.
- **Banking Licenses (if applicable)**: Licenses required in jurisdictions where the entity operates as a bank.
- **Skilled Financial Workforce**: Staff for banking, treasury, and risk functions.
- **Cash & Liquid Reserves**: Liquid assets to meet deposit withdrawals and settlement.

## Risks — topics to cover

- **Credit Risk (on internal loans/deposits)**: Risk of borrower default or inability to return deposits.
- **Interest Rate Mismatch Risk**: Risk from maturity or repricing mismatches between deposits and loans.
- **FX Risk**: Risk from FX positions taken on behalf of group entities.
- **Liquidity & Settlement Risk**: Risk of being unable to settle payments or meet withdrawals.
- **Operational Risk**: Risk from system failures, fraud, or process errors.
- **Regulatory Risk (banking supervision in some jurisdictions)**: Risk from banking regulation and licensing requirements.

## Typical transaction types

- Loan arrangements (as lender/borrower)
- Cash pooling
- Services (payment/FX services)
