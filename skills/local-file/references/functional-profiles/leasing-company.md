# Leasing Company

Slug: `leasing-company`

## Profile overview
Acquires assets (equipment, vehicles, property) and leases them to group entities. Bears residual value risk and manages the asset lifecycle.

## Functions — topics to cover
- **Asset Acquisition & Procurement**: How the entity identifies, evaluates, and purchases assets for the lease portfolio.
- **Lease Contract Administration**: How lease terms are negotiated, documented, and monitored across the group.
- **Asset Maintenance Coordination**: How maintenance schedules, repairs, and service coordination are managed.
- **Residual Value Assessment**: How end-of-lease asset values are estimated and tracked.
- **Lease Renewal & Termination**: How renewals, buyouts, and returns are processed and documented.
- **Asset Disposal**: How off-lease assets are sold, remarketed, or scrapped.
- **Financial Reporting & Compliance**: How lease accounting, tax, and regulatory reporting are handled.

## Assets — topics to cover
- **Leased Equipment & Vehicles**: The physical assets held in the leasing portfolio and their composition.
- **Leased Property & Facilities**: Real estate or fixed assets under lease arrangements.
- **Lease Receivables**: Outstanding amounts owed by lessees under active contracts.
- **Asset Management Systems**: IT and processes used to track and manage the asset lifecycle.
- **Financial Reserves (for residual value)**: Capital or provisions set aside to cover residual value shortfalls.
- **Skilled Asset Management Workforce**: People and expertise needed to run leasing operations.

## Risks — topics to cover
- **Residual Value & Obsolescence Risk**: Exposure to assets being worth less than expected at end of lease.
- **Credit Risk (lessee default)**: Risk that group lessees fail to pay rent or return assets.
- **Interest Rate Risk (if financed)**: Impact of rate changes on lease financing costs.
- **Asset Damage & Insurance Risk**: Physical damage, loss, or inadequate insurance coverage.
- **Market Risk (on asset values)**: Fluctuations in second-hand market prices for leased assets.
- **Regulatory Risk**: Changes in lease accounting, tax, or industry regulation.

## Typical transaction types
- Services (leasing services)
- Loan arrangements (if financing the lease)
