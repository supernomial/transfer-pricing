# Active Holding

Slug: `active-holding`

## Profile overview
Manages and controls subsidiary operations — provides strategic direction, allocates capital, and coordinates group activities. More than a passive shareholder; performs real management and governance functions.

## Functions — topics to cover
- **Strategic Planning & Direction**: How group strategy is set, communicated, and aligned across subsidiaries.
- **Capital Allocation & Investment Decisions**: How capital is allocated, investments approved, and funding decisions made.
- **Group Coordination & Governance**: How subsidiaries are coordinated, policies applied, and governance enforced.
- **Performance Monitoring of Subsidiaries**: How subsidiary performance is tracked, reviewed, and acted upon.
- **M&A Activities**: Role in acquisitions, disposals, and corporate restructuring.
- **Risk Management & Compliance Oversight**: How group-level risk and compliance are supervised.
- **Financial Reporting & Consolidation**: How group accounts are prepared, consolidated, and reported.
- **Board & Management Appointment**: How subsidiary boards and key management are selected and governed.

## Assets — topics to cover
- **Shareholdings in Subsidiaries**: The equity investments and control structures.
- **Management Know-how & Governance IP**: Strategic, governance, and management intangibles.
- **Strategic Workforce (senior management)**: Key people providing group-level direction.
- **IT & Reporting Systems**: Systems used for consolidation, reporting, and group coordination.
- **Office Facilities**: Premises for headquarters and management activities.
- **Financial Resources**: Capital and liquidity for group-level activities.

## Risks — topics to cover
- **Strategic & Investment Risk**: Exposure from strategic and capital allocation decisions.
- **Subsidiary Performance Risk**: Dependence on subsidiary results for group outcomes.
- **Regulatory & Compliance Risk**: Group-wide regulatory and governance obligations.
- **Reputation Risk**: Brand and reputational exposure at group level.
- **Key Person Risk**: Reliance on senior management for group direction.
- **Market Risk (if actively managing business units)**: Market exposure from managed operations.

## Typical transaction types
- Services (management/advisory services to subsidiaries)
- Financial guarantees (guaranteeing subsidiary debt)
- Loan arrangements (intercompany lending)
