# Cash Pool Leader

Slug: `cash-pool-leader`

## Profile overview

Manages the group's cash pooling arrangement — concentrates cash from participants, manages liquidity, and allocates interest. Acts as intermediary between the group and external banks.

## Functions — topics to cover

- **Cash Concentration & Sweeping**: How excess cash is swept from participant accounts into the pool and made available for group use.
- **Liquidity Management & Forecasting**: How the leader forecasts cash needs, maintains liquidity buffers, and manages shortfalls.
- **Interest Allocation & Calculation**: How interest is calculated and allocated to participants (credit/debit rates, formulas, timing).
- **Bank Relationship Management**: How the leader interfaces with external banks, negotiates facilities, and manages pool accounts.
- **Cash Position Reporting**: How the leader reports aggregate pool position and individual participant balances to treasury and management.
- **FX Management (if multi-currency pool)**: How multi-currency pools handle FX exposure, conversion, or sub-pools.
- **Participant Account Monitoring**: How the leader tracks participant positions, limits, and compliance with pool terms.

## Assets — topics to cover

- **Cash & Bank Balances**: Pooled cash held in external bank accounts and working balances.
- **Treasury Management Systems**: TMS or equivalent used for sweeping, allocation, and reporting.
- **Banking Relationships & Credit Facilities**: External overdraft, credit lines, or committed facilities backing the pool.
- **Financial Modeling Tools**: Tools used for liquidity forecasting and scenario analysis.
- **Treasury Workforce**: Staff responsible for pool operations and oversight.

## Risks — topics to cover

- **Liquidity Risk**: Risk that pool cannot meet participant withdrawals or bank obligations.
- **Interest Rate Risk**: Risk from rate changes affecting credit/debit margins or external facility costs.
- **Counterparty/Credit Risk (vis-à-vis participants and banks)**: Risk of participant or bank default.
- **FX Risk (multi-currency pools)**: Risk from currency movements in multi-currency arrangements.
- **Operational & Settlement Risk**: Risk from system failures or settlement delays.
- **Regulatory Risk**: Risk from changes in cash pooling regulation or bank supervision.

## Typical transaction types

- Cash pooling (as pool leader)
- Loan arrangements (if on-lending)
