# Captive Insurance Entity

Slug: `captive-insurance-entity`

## Profile overview

Provides insurance coverage to group entities — underwrites risks that would otherwise be placed with external insurers. May retain risk or reinsure externally.

## Functions — topics to cover

- **Risk Assessment & Underwriting**: How risks are assessed, priced, and accepted or declined.
- **Premium Calculation & Collection**: How premiums are calculated and collected from insured entities.
- **Claims Processing & Settlement**: How claims are filed, evaluated, and paid.
- **Reinsurance Arrangement & Management**: How external reinsurance is arranged and managed.
- **Policy Administration**: How policies are issued, renewed, and administered.
- **Reserve Management**: How technical provisions and reserves are established and maintained.
- **Actuarial Analysis**: How actuaries support pricing, reserving, and capital modeling.
- **Regulatory Compliance & Reporting**: How the captive complies with insurance regulation and reporting requirements.

## Assets — topics to cover

- **Insurance Reserves & Technical Provisions**: Reserves held for unpaid claims and unearned premiums.
- **Investment Portfolio**: Assets backing reserves and capital.
- **Actuarial Models & Systems**: Models for pricing, reserving, and risk assessment.
- **Skilled Insurance Workforce**: Underwriters, claims handlers, actuaries, and compliance staff.
- **Reinsurance Contracts**: Contracts with reinsurers for risk transfer.
- **IT Systems**: Systems for policy administration, claims, and reporting.

## Risks — topics to cover

- **Underwriting & Claims Risk**: Risk of adverse claims experience vs. premium assumptions.
- **Reserving Risk (adequacy of provisions)**: Risk that reserves are insufficient for future claims.
- **Investment Risk (on reserves)**: Risk from changes in value of reserve investments.
- **Catastrophe & Accumulation Risk**: Risk from large or correlated losses.
- **Reinsurance Counterparty Risk**: Risk that reinsurers default on their obligations.
- **Regulatory & Solvency Risk**: Risk from regulatory changes or failure to meet solvency requirements.
