# Central Financing Hub

Slug: `central-financing-hub`

## Profile overview

Centralized treasury entity that raises external funding and on-lends to group entities. May also manage hedging, guarantees, and financial risk on behalf of the group.

## Functions — topics to cover

- **External Debt Raising & Capital Markets Access**: How the hub raises debt (bonds, syndicated loans, bilateral facilities) for the group.
- **Intercompany Lending & Borrowing**: How the hub lends to group entities and borrows from them as needed.
- **Interest Rate Management & Hedging**: How the hub manages interest rate exposure and uses derivatives or structured products.
- **Credit Risk Assessment of Group Entities**: How the hub evaluates creditworthiness of borrower entities.
- **Cash Flow Forecasting & Management**: How the hub forecasts group cash flows and manages liquidity.
- **Financial Reporting & Compliance**: How the hub reports to lenders, regulators, and group management.
- **Guarantee Issuance & Management**: How the hub issues or arranges guarantees for group entities.

## Assets — topics to cover

- **Loan Receivables (intercompany)**: Loans extended to group entities.
- **Cash & Liquid Investments**: Liquid assets for liquidity management and debt service.
- **Financial Systems & Risk Models**: Systems for loan administration, risk monitoring, and reporting.
- **Banking Relationships**: Relationships with banks, investors, and capital markets.
- **Skilled Treasury Workforce**: Staff for treasury, credit, and risk management.
- **Hedging Instruments**: Derivatives and other instruments for interest rate and FX hedging.

## Risks — topics to cover

- **Credit Risk (on intercompany loans)**: Risk of default by borrowing group entities.
- **Interest Rate Risk**: Risk from changes in market rates affecting margins or hedge effectiveness.
- **FX Risk**: Risk from currency movements on foreign-currency debt or receivables.
- **Liquidity Risk**: Risk of being unable to meet debt service or fund new loans.
- **Refinancing Risk**: Risk of being unable to refinance maturing external debt.
- **Regulatory & Compliance Risk**: Risk from capital, reporting, or tax regulations.
- **Market Risk (on investments)**: Risk from changes in value of liquid investments.

## Typical transaction types

- Loan arrangements (as lender)
- Financial guarantees (as guarantor)
- Cash pooling (may overlap)
