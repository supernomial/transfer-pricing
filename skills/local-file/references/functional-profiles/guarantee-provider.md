# Guarantee Provider

Slug: `guarantee-provider`

## Profile overview

Issues financial or performance guarantees on behalf of group entities — enabling them to access external credit, win contracts, or meet regulatory requirements.

## Functions — topics to cover

- **Credit Assessment of Guaranteed Entities**: How the guarantor assesses the creditworthiness of entities requesting guarantees.
- **Guarantee Issuance & Documentation**: How guarantees are structured, documented, and issued.
- **Monitoring of Guaranteed Obligations**: How the guarantor monitors the underlying obligations and triggers.
- **Reserve & Capital Management**: How reserves or capital are held to support guarantee exposure.
- **Fee Calculation**: How guarantee fees are calculated and charged.
- **Default Management & Recovery**: How the guarantor responds to defaults and pursues recovery.
- **Regulatory Compliance**: How the guarantor complies with capital, reporting, and regulatory requirements for guarantee activities.

## Assets — topics to cover

- **Capital Reserves (backing guarantees)**: Capital or reserves held to support guarantee exposure.
- **Credit Analysis Models**: Models and processes for assessing counterparty credit risk.
- **Guarantee Documentation**: Master agreements, individual guarantees, and related legal documentation.
- **Financial Systems**: Systems for tracking exposure, fees, and reporting.
- **Skilled Credit Workforce**: Staff for credit analysis, documentation, and monitoring.

## Risks — topics to cover

- **Default & Credit Risk (guaranteed entity fails to pay)**: Risk that the guaranteed entity defaults and the guarantor must pay.
- **Concentration Risk (large exposures)**: Risk from large or concentrated guarantee exposures.
- **Regulatory & Capital Risk**: Risk from regulatory capital or leverage requirements.
- **Recovery Risk**: Risk of being unable to recover amounts paid under guarantees from the guaranteed entity.
- **Reputation Risk**: Risk from association with defaulting entities or guarantee disputes.

## Typical transaction types

- Financial guarantees (as guarantor)
- Performance guarantees (as guarantor)
